Metadata Summary

Originator Division of Parks and Recreation
Abstract Legislative statutory boundaries for sixty six state parks, six state recreation areas, and eight state waysides. These data are derived principally from DNR's pls40ne3 coverage where available, with special delineations using heads up digitizing from DOQ and DRGs with interepretations based on changes in land use. Other sources include plsscne3, BOE digital parcel lines, survey quality GPS, and best guess.
Use Tips Mapped from a variety of sources, using the most accurate information available. Layer is regularly updated.
Browse Graphic View a sample of the data.
Time Period of Content Date 2002
Currentness Reference This data is constantly being improved as more accurate data sets are completed for various areas of the state. These boundaries will be revisited annually as legislation for parks statutory boundaries are changed. Individual boundary segments have dates that they were updated. Some segments have not been updated from the original set. These .aat segments have a bdry_date of 19900101.

Section 2 Data Quality Information Top of full metadata Top of page
Attribute Accuracy Source table BDRY_STPRKPY3.DBF: Not specifically assesed.
Logical Consistency Data are topologically correct using ARC/INFO. All polygons are closed and lines intersect where intended.
Completeness Data are complete over the entire state.
Horizontal Positional Accuracy Source layer bdry_stprkpy3: Horizontal positional accuracy is dependant on accuracy of the pl40ne3 dataset and statutory descriptions of park boundaries. In most areas, pls40ne3 features can be expected to be +/- 40 feet from their true position. The most significant problems have typically been observed in the northeastern part of the state. However, when significant accuracy problems are observed, State Parks Statuatory boundaries will typically rely on other sources of information, such as cultural features visible on aerial imagery. Accurate land surveyed boundaries are also used on occasion.
